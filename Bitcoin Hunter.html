⚠ Legal & Ethical Disclaimer
This tool is provided for educational and research purposes only. Scanning private key spaces and checking balances against live Bitcoin addresses is a computationally futile exercise — the 256-bit keyspace is astronomically large. The probability of finding a funded wallet by chance is effectively zero.

You must not attempt to access, transfer, or claim funds from Bitcoin addresses that do not belong to you. Doing so may constitute unauthorised computer access or theft under the laws of your jurisdiction.

The Bitcoin Puzzle transaction is a publicly known research challenge with intentionally constrained key ranges. All other scanning is strictly for cryptographic research.
API Rate Limits
■ blockchain.info — ~300 req/5min direct (best for batches)
■ blockcypher — ~200 req/hour free tier (strict, use sparingly)
■ blockchair — ~30 req/min free tier
Tip: Use 1–2s delay with batch=3 for sustainable scanning. Run ⚡ Test APIs first to see which sources are reachable.
Scan Modes Explained
■ Sequential — starts at key #1, increments +1. Position saved to localStorage.
■ Random — cryptographically random keys across the full 256-bit space.
■ Puzzle Mode — targets the Bitcoin Puzzle transaction addresses (confirmed funded, known ranges). Select a puzzle from the grid above. Scans randomly within the specific bit-range of that puzzle.
■ Era 2009–2013 — targets patterns from early Bitcoin Core wallet generation. Mixes low sequential keys, brainwallet patterns, and time-seeded pseudo-random values mimicking early C++ PRNG behaviour.
About the Bitcoin Puzzle
In 2015 someone funded 256 Bitcoin addresses. Each puzzle #N has its private key constrained to N bits (range: 2^(N-1) to 2^N - 1). Puzzles #1–70 and some others have been solved. Remaining unsolved puzzles like #71, #72, #75... each hold their number in BTC (e.g. #71 = 7.1 BTC, #75 = 7.5 BTC). They are the best-known publicly funded addresses with mathematically constrained key ranges — making targeted scanning realistic for lower puzzles.
